UC Irvine Learning and Academic Resource Center (LARC)

Irvine, CA | Mentor, Tutor | September 2022 - June 2025

For 6 academic quarters as a "Tutorial Leader," I held 1-hour tutorial sessions independently with various numbers of students. On average, I would have around 3-5 students enrolled per tutorial. I supported two beginner Python programming classes at UCI, ICS 31 and ICS 32. I also held review sessions when students had exams or projects.

I was promoted to the "Mentor" role in my second year at LARC, where I helped co-lead a staff meeting, host a social event, evaluate tutorial sessions, and rewrite the Zoom training documentation.

Due to scheduling conflicts, I stepped down from the "Tutorial Leader" and "Mentor" roles in my third and final year at LARC, but continued to support LARC as a "LARC After Dark" tutor. In that role, I helped students in weekly, 2 hour drop-in sessions, and often answered questions using the whiteboard to visualize concepts. I helped students debug their projects thanks to this.

I was hired for LARC right after changing majors to computer science and after only having 4 programming classes of experience, so I was still very new with programming and would be honest with students. A lot of students related with this and asked me for advice. I was also open about discussing when I repeated classes, as some of my students also related to that.

For every tutorial, I made original worksheets based on the instructor's lesson plans and my learning style. You can view examples of my best ones here. (Fun fact: the conditional statement worksheet's first draft was what I used for this job's interview.)

I also helped with redesigning and updating the Zoom tutorial training documentation, based on style guidelines I was asked to follow (such as using bookmarks).

To help students participate actively, I explained concepts and guided them with questions directly on the whiteboard, and then they would try coding questions on their computer. After tutorial sessions, I would upload answer keys with additional information.

I also created lesson plans for myself to manage my time. Given the 1-hour time constraint, I eventually simplified my worksheet and checked in with students directly to avoid overspending time on the guided practice and icebreaker.

Most of my students were in their first year of college, changing majors, or new to programming, so they were often under stress. To help them relax, I often brought candy and Play-Doh to tutorial sessions.

I am honored to say that due to my involvement at LARC, I was given the "Helper of the People" award in the Discord server that many UCI computer science students use for networking and planning.

I am also in a few social media posts, as I often volunteered my time to promote the activities I participated in. You can see my Instagram post here.


OC Fair & Event Center

Costa Mesa, CA | Carnival Ticket Seller | July 2025 - August 2025

For the entire 2025 OC Fair, I sold tickets at various booths at night. Every day was different because of the timing, location of the booth, and whether the booth accepted cash or not.

Being interested in business and hospitality, I wanted to get more experience with it as well as with cash handling. This job helped me obtain those skills quickly. I learned how to multitask, work in a fast-paced environment, and respond to customer complaints while maintaining an accurate balance. I also learned from my mistakes and setbacks quickly. For instance, after being $0.50 short of change for a customer, I learned how to be prepared for payments with coins and have since achieved a perfect balance after every shift.

I am also now familiar with using a POS system and ticket printer, as well as checking for counterfeit money.

Yes, I don't think you'd expect a recent computer science graduate to do this as a first job since graduation. I actually started this job as a student; I was finishing my last two GE classes in online summer school to get my degree. I finished my degree halfway through this job.

However, I really wanted to do this job, as I feel like knowing these skills are fundamental to future business roles. I didn't do it as a high school or college student, so I might as well do it now.

Beverly Hills, CA | Business Affairs/Production Intern | January 2025 - June 2025

This saved a lot of time in populating the spreadsheet and allowed me to focus on getting the ISRC codes from the OpenPlay catalog. In case APG uses it, I gave them the code and wrote a README file to explain how to use the program.
